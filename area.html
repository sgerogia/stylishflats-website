The Neighbourhood

The neighbourhood is safe and quiet, with a number of supermarkets (Lidl, Tesco), convenience stores and restaurants all at walking distance. A short 5’ walk takes you to Canary Wharf where you will be spoilt for choice when it comes to shopping and entertainment.
East London and the Docklands is THE definite area to explore and discover in London. Old riverside warehouses (wharves) stand side-by-side with awe-inspiring new buildings and towers, all paying tribute to the entrepreneurial spirit and energy of London.
In the previous centuries the surrounding area was a massive port facility, where ships from all over the world were bringing their exotic cargo. This legacy lives to this day in the various location names: West India Quay, East India Dock, Tobacco Dock,… A short stroll in the nearby areas of Wapping, Limehouse and Isle of Dogs will give you the chance to walk in stone-cobbled streets and admire a number of historic buildings and wharves (from Georgian to Victorian and even older), currently bustling with life.
Just a small selection of the nearby sites:

  • Limehouse Marina: With its impressive selection of live-in yachts and barges and the nearby pub The Narrow owned by celebrity chef Gordon Ramsey.
  • Wapping: The cobbled streets of which will make you forget you are in London.
    • Do not forget to enjoy a pint of ale at The Grapes (look for the original staff of Gandalf from Lord of The Rings behind the bar) or at the Prospect of Whitby (running continuously as a pub for 500 years!)
  • Canary Wharf: The bank towers (One Canada Square is the 2nd tallest in Europe), luxury designer shopping and restaurants will make you think you are in New York. Hard to believe that most of the buildings are actually built on water. Do give a visit to the rooftop “jungle” of the Crossrail station.
  • West India Quay and Millwall Docks: The old port cranes, the newly built residential towers and the serenity of the water make for a dramatic contrast.
  • Riverside walk: Probably the best part of the area in my opinion, you can choose to have a mind-clearing walk or jog all the way to Tower Bridge and Tower of London. Or towards the opposite direction, towards Greenwich, walking under the river via the foot tunnel. In either direction you will enjoy some of the most peaceful and beautiful views of London.
  • Docklands Museum: In its many floors shows the evolution of the area. The highlight is the recreation of a Victorian dock alleyway. You cannot help but expect Jack the Ripper to show up around the corner.
  • Billingsgate Market: Worth waking up early (closes at 08:00) to see the hustle and bustle of London’s central fish market. You can have a fisherman’s breakfast (grilled kippers and tea) in one of the cafes and, why not, get a fresh fish or some oysters to enjoy back at the flat.

Getting Around

The location is ideal to move around London fast and without fuss.

Whether you want to move around central London (Tower Bridge, St Paul’s, British Museum,…), explore Greenwich, catch a train or Eurostar to mainland or attend an exhibition in Excel, everything is a few tube stops, bus stops or a walk away.
From Westferry DLR you can be in:

  • Central London: 8 min
  • Tower of London: 8 min
  • Greenwich: 15 min
  • The Olympic village in Stratford: 15 min
  • St. Paul’s: 15 min
  • Covent Garden: 30 min
  • Shoreditch: 20 min
Finally it’s worth mentioning two special ways of moving around London, both in very close proximity to the flat
  • River boat: From the Canary Wharf pier, you can make your commute to central London a site-seeing cruise.
  • Cycle Hire: Definitely the cheapest way to move around London. There are 3 Cycle Hire stands close by. You can pick-up and drop-off at 100s of locations across the city for only £2 / day.
